How to Use This Revision Guide

Why these words matter

Adverbs, particles, and simple connecting words form the "connective tissue" of Japanese sentences. While nouns and verbs provide the core topic, these words dictate when, how often, to what degree, and in what direction actions take place.

💡 Exam Insight: JLPT N5 grammar and listening questions frequently hinge on understanding these exact vocabulary items. Missing a single adverb like まだ (not yet) or しか (only) can completely flip the meaning of a sentence!

01 / Adverbs

Time Adverbs (時間副詞)

Time adverbs specify when an action occurs, its immediacy, or its state of completion.

Japanese Romaji English Example Sentence Memory Aid / Trap
もう mou already / anymore (+ negative)
もう昼ご飯を食べました。
I have already eaten lunch.
 Sounds like "Moo!" - The cow has already eaten grass.
まだ mada not yet (+ negative) / still
宿題はまだ終わっていません。
My homework is not yet finished.
 Sounds like "Madder" - My teacher gets madder because I am not yet done.
もうすぐ mousugu soon / shortly
もうすぐ電車が来ます。
The train will come soon.
 "Mou" (already) + "sugu" (immediately) = Coming very soon.
すぐ / すぐに sugu / sugu ni immediately / right away
すぐに行きます！
I will go immediately!
 Think: "Soon-goo". Do it immediately, no delay!
さっき sakki a while ago / just now
さっき田中さんに会いました。
I met Mr. Tanaka a while ago.
 "Sakki" refers to a very recent past (minutes or hours ago).
あとで ato de later / afterwards
あとで電話をします。
I will call you later.
 "Ato" means behind/after. "Ato de" = later on.
02 / Frequency

Frequency Words (頻度副詞)

Frequency adverbs range from 100% (always) to 0% (never). Note which ones strictly require negative verb forms!

Japanese Romaji Frequency & Meaning Example Sentence Grammar Requirement
いつも itsumo 100% always
朝はいつもコーヒーを飲みます。
I always drink coffee in the morning.
 Positive Verbs
よく yoku 80% often / well
日本のアニメをよく見ます。
I often watch Japanese anime.
 Positive Verbs
ときどき tokidoki 50% sometimes
ときどき図書館で勉強します。
I sometimes study at the library.
 Positive Verbs
Repeat symbol (々) means "doki-doki" double.
たいてい taitei 70% usually / mostly
日曜日はたいてい家にいます。
On Sundays, I am usually at home.
 Positive Verbs
あまり amari 20% not very / not much
私はお酒をあまり飲みません。
I do not drink alcohol very much.
 MUST use Negative
Amari + Neg = "Not very..."
ぜんぜん zenzen 0% not at all / never
英語がぜんぜんわかりません。
I do not understand English at all.
 MUST use Negative
Zenzen + Neg = "Not at all..."
03 / Degree

Degree Words (程度副詞)

Degree words modify adjectives or verbs to specify intensity or comparison level.

Japanese Romaji English Meaning Example Sentence Context Note
とても totemo very
このラーメンはとても美味しいです。
This ramen is very delicious.
 Standard way to say "very". Works with positive adjectives.
たいへん taihen very / awfully / tough
日本語の文法はたいへん難しいです。
Japanese grammar is extremely/very difficult.
 More formal/written than とても. Also means "tough situation".
すこし sukoshi a little / slightly
日本語がすこし話せます。
I can speak a little Japanese.
 Polite/written version of "a little".
ちょっと chotto a bit / just a moment
ちょっと待ってください。
Please wait a moment/little bit.
 More casual. Used frequently to politely decline requests.
もっと motto more
もっとゆっくり話してください。
Please speak more slowly.
 Think: "Give me more, like a motto".
いちばん ichiban most / number one
くだものの中でりんごがいちばん好きです。
Among fruits, I like apples the most.
 "Ichi" (one) + "ban" (number) = Best / Most.
04 / Comparison

Comparison Words (比較表現)

Essential structures for comparing things in Japanese. Focus on the word order and particle roles!

Formula 1: Direct Comparison

A より B のほうが [Adjective] です。

Meaning: B is more [Adjective] than A.

猫より犬のほうが好きです。
I like dogs more than cats. (Dogs > Cats)

Formula 2: Asking Preferences

A と B とどちらが [Adjective] ですか。

Meaning: Between A and B, which one is more [Adjective]?

肉と魚とどちらが好きですか。
Between meat and fish, which do you prefer?
Japanese Romaji English Meaning Usage Example Core Concept
より yori than
東京は京都より大きいです。
Tokyo is bigger than Kyoto.
 Acts as the starting point benchmark (than X).
ほうが hou ga more (preferred option)
こちらのほうが安いです。
This side is more cheap (cheaper).
 "Hou" means direction/alternative. B's direction is better.
おなじ onaji same
彼とおなじ時計を買いました。
I bought the same watch as him.
 Shows equivalence. Usually takes "to" (same as: A とおなじ).
いちばん ichiban most / number one
日本で富士山がいちばん高いです。
Mount Fuji is the most high (highest) in Japan.
 Used to express superlative (the absolute most).
05 / Quantity

Quantity Words (数量表現)

These words limit, approximate, or define quantities. Look out for the positive vs. negative grammar restrictions!

Japanese Romaji Meaning Example Sentence Grammar Rules
ぐらい / くらい gurai / kurai approx. (duration/amount)
１時間ぐらい泳ぎました。
I swam for about 1 hour.
 Used for duration, distance, money, and volumes.
ごろ goro approx. (point in time)
３時ごろに駅で会いましょう。
Let's meet at the station around 3 o'clock.
 Specific Points
Cannot be used for durations.
だけ dake only / just
ひらがなだけ書けます。
I can write only Hiragana.
 Positive Verbs
Simple limits.
しか shika only (nothing else)
財布に100円しかありません。
I have only 100 yen (nothing else) in my wallet.
 MUST use Negative
Adds a feeling of insufficiency.
ぜんぶ zenbu all / everything
宿題はぜんぶ終わりました。
My homework is all finished.
 "Zen" (all) + "bu" (parts) = Everything.
みんな minna everyone / all (people/things)
みんなで歌いましょう。
Let's sing all together.
 Usually refers to people, but can mean entire group sets.
06 / Questions

Question Words (疑問詞)

Essential question words to seek information in JLPT N5 listening and reading scripts.

Japanese Romaji English Example Sentence Common Combination
なに / なん nani / nan what
これはなんですか。
What is this?
 なにを (What [obj]) / 何曜日 (What day)
だれ dare who
あの人はだれですか。
Who is that person?
 だれと (With whom) / だれの (Whose)
どこ doko where
お手洗いはどこですか。
Where is the restroom?
 どこに (Where at) / どこから (Where from)
いつ itsu when
テストはいつですか。
When is the test?
 Does NOT require the time particle "ni" directly.
なぜ naze why
なぜ遅れましたか。
Why are you late? (Polite/Formal)
 More formal than どうして. Used in written exams.
どうして doushite why / how come
どうして学校を休みましたか。
Why did you skip school?
 Frequently used in N5 conversations.
どう dou how
日本語の勉強はどうですか。
How is studying Japanese?
 どうやって (How/By what method)
07 / Indefinite

Nani-Series & Dare-Series (不定代名詞)

By adding か (indefinite) or も (inclusive/negative), basic question words turn into key expressions.

Question Word + か → "Some-..." (Positive verb)
Question Word + も → "No-..." (Negative verb only)
Japanese Romaji Meaning Example Sentence Grammar Check
なにか nanika something
カバンの中になにかありますか。
Is there something inside the bag?
 Positive Verbs
なにも nanimo nothing
冷蔵庫にはなにもありません。
There is nothing in the refrigerator.
 MUST use Negative
だれか dareka someone
教室にだれかいますか。
Is someone in the classroom?
 Positive Verbs
だれも daremo no one / nobody
部屋にはだれもいません。
There is no one in the room.
 MUST use Negative
どこか dokoka somewhere
週末にどこかへ行きましたか。
Did you go somewhere over the weekend?
 Positive Verbs
どこも dokomo nowhere
昨日はどこも行きませんでした。
I went nowhere (didn't go anywhere) yesterday.
 MUST use Negative
08 / Connectors

Connecting Words (接続詞)

Connectors bridge sentences, indicate causality, or introduce contrasting ideas.

Japanese Romaji Function & Meaning Example Sentence Grammar Role
そして soshite and / then
朝起きました。そして顔を洗いました。
I woke up in the morning. And then washed my face.
 Links sentences chronologically or details additions.
それから sorekara and then / after that
宿題をして、それからテレビを見ました。
I did my homework, and after that watched TV.
 "Sore" (that) + "kara" (from) = From that point onwards.
だから dakara therefore / so
雨が降っています。だから傘を持ちます。
It is raining. Therefore, I carry an umbrella.
 Introduces results caused by the preceding sentence.
でも demo but / however
旅行に行きたいです。でもお金がありません。
I want to go on a trip. But I have no money.
 Starts a sentence to introduce contrast. (Conversational)
しかし shikashi however / but
努力しました。しかし負けました。
I made an effort. However, I lost.
 Formal/written contrast. Very common in N5 reading sections.
が ga but / although
薬を飲みましたが、熱が下がりません。
I took medicine but my fever won't go down.
 Sentence Mid-Connector
Glues two clauses together directly.
09 / Proximity

Location & Direction Words (これ・それ・あれ)

The legendary "Ko-So-A-Do" matrix. Understanding proximity is key to answering N5 visual maps and audio exams.

これ / ここ / こちら

Ko-Series

🏠 **Close to Speaker**

ここ (koko)Here
こちら (kochira)This way (polite)

「ここは私の教室です」 (Here is my classroom.)

それ / そこ / そちら

So-Series

👥 **Close to Listener**

そこ (soko)There
そちら (sochira)That way (polite)

「そこに辞書があります」 (There is a dictionary near you.)

あれ / あそこ / あちら

A-Series

🌳 **Far from Both**

あそこ (asoko)Over there
あちら (achira)Way over there

「トイレはあそこです」 (Restroom is over there.)

どれ / どちら / どこ

Do-Series

❓ **Question / Choice**

どこ (doko)Where
どちら (dochira)Which way / Who

「駅はどちらですか」 (Which way is the station?)

Japanese Romaji Meaning Example Sentence Politeness Note
ここ / そこ / あそこ koko / soko / asoko here / there / over there
郵便局はあそこにあります。
The post office is over there.
 Standard physical location indicators.
こちら / そちら / あちら kochira / sochira / achira this way / that way / over there
こちらへどうぞ。
Please come this way.
 Polite directions. Can also introduce people (e.g. This is Mr. X).
どちら dochira which way / which one
お国はどちらですか。
Which country are you from? (Polite)
 Polite form of どこ (where) or どれ (which one). Choice of two.
10 / Core Adverbs

High-Frequency JLPT N5 Adverbs (必須一般副詞)

These general adverbs describe quality of actions, probability, or subjective desires.

Japanese Romaji English Meaning Example Sentence Exam Hint & Usage
だんだん dandan gradually / step-by-step
暖かくなって、だんだん春になります。
It's getting warmer; it is gradually becoming spring.
 Like walking down a flight of steps: Dan-dan. Used with change verbs.
ゆっくり yukkuri slowly / at ease
週末は家でゆっくり休みたい。
I want to rest leisurely at home on the weekend.
 Do not confuse with "fast". Represents relaxation or low speed.
はやく hayaku quickly / early
明日はやく起きなければなりません。
I must wake up early tomorrow.
 Can mean early (早く) or fast (速く) depending on kanji context.
いっしょに issho ni together
いっしょにご飯を食べませんか。
Won't we eat dinner together?
 Frequently paired with ~mashou (let's) or ~masen ka (won't we?).
たぶん tabun probably / perhaps
明日はたぶん雨でしょう。
Tomorrow it will probably rain.
 Often pairs with ~deshou or ~to omoimasu (I think...). Shows ~70% guess.
ぜひ zehi by all means / definitely
日本へぜひ行きたいです。
I definitely want to go to Japan.
 Pairs with ~tai (want to do) or ~てください (please do). Strong desire.
なかなか nakanaka not easily / not readily
バスがなかなか来ません。
The bus is not easily coming (taking a very long time).
 MUST use Negative
Indicates delay or difficulty in completion.
もちろん mochiron of course / naturally
もちろん宿題をやりましたよ。
Of course I did my homework!
 Indicates obvious truth or permission.
ほんとうに hontou ni really / truly
今日はほんとうに寒いです。
Today is really cold.
 Emphasizes truth. From "hontou" (truth).

12 / Warning

Common Exam Traps (試験の罠)

Beware of these five tricky pairings that JLPT examiners frequently use to try and catch you out!

ぐらい vs ごろ (About vs. Around)

ぐらい (gurai)

Duration / Quantity: Used with time spans, money, distances.

３時間ぐらい勉強した。
Studied for about 3 hours.

ごろ (goro)

Point in time: Used with specific clock times, months, years.

３時ごろに起きる。
Wake up around 3 o'clock.

もう vs まだ (Already vs. Not Yet)

もう (mou)

Action is completed. Frequently used with past tense verbs.

もう食べました。
I have already eaten.

まだ (mada)

Action is uncompleted. Strictly used with negative verbs (~te inai).

まだ食べていません。
I haven't eaten yet.

より vs ほうが (Comparative Roles)

Remember the core balance formula: A より B のほうが...

[A] より = Less preferred marker
"Compared to A..."
[B] ほうが = More preferred option
"B is more..."

Negative Verbs Requirement (あまり / ぜんぜん)

Adverbs like あまり (not very), ぜんぜん (not at all), and なかなか (not easily) MUST be followed by negative verbs.

あまり / ぜんぜん / なかなか + [Negative Verb] (〜ない / 〜ません)
❌ ぜんぜん分かります (Incorrect)
✅ ぜんぜん分かりません (Correct - "I don't understand at all")

Grammar Guide 1

State & Being (状態・存在)

〜です / 〜ではありません
To be / Not to be (Present)
[Noun/Na-Adj] です
[Noun/Na-Adj] ではありません
私は学生です。
I am a student.
彼は先生ではありません。
He is not a teacher.
💡 ではありません is formal. じゃありません is used in daily speaking.
〜でした / 〜ではありませんでした
Was / Was not (Past)
[Noun/Na-Adj] でした
[Noun/Na-Adj] ではありませんでした
きのうは雨でした。
It was raining yesterday.
テストは簡単ではありませんでした。
The test was not easy.
💡 Past tense of the copula. Watch out for I-adjectives which conjugate differently (〜かったです).
〜があります / 〜います
There is / To have (Existence)
[Place] に [Thing] があります
[Place] に [Person/Animal] がいます
机の上に本があります。
There is a book on the desk.
あそこに犬がいます。
There is a dog over there.
💡 あります is for inanimate objects. います is for living, moving things.
Grammar Guide 2

Movement & Requests (移動・依頼)

〜へ行きます / 〜に行きます
Go to ~
[Place] へ/に 行きます / 来ます / 帰ります
明日、東京へ行きます。
I will go to Tokyo tomorrow.
うちへ帰ります。
I will return home.
💡 へ emphasizes direction, に emphasizes destination. Both are mostly interchangeable for N5.
〜をください
Please give me ~
[Noun] をください
水をください。
Please give me water.
りんごを３つください。
Please give me three apples.
💡 Used when ordering at restaurants or asking for items in a store.
〜てください
Please do ~
[Verb て-form] ください
ちょっと待ってください。
Please wait a moment.
ここに名前を書いてください。
Please write your name here.
💡 Standard polite request. Requires mastering the Verb て-form.
Grammar Guide 3

Suggestions & Permissions (提案・許可)

〜ましょう / 〜ませんか
Let's ~ / Won't we ~?
[Verb stem] ましょう
[Verb stem] ませんか
一緒に昼ご飯を食べましょう。
Let's eat lunch together.
コーヒーを飲みませんか。
Won't you drink some coffee?
💡 ませんか is softer and more polite as it gives the listener room to refuse.
〜てもいいです
May I ~ / It is okay to ~
[Verb て-form] もいいです
ここで写真を撮ってもいいですか。
May I take a picture here?
このペンを使ってもいいです。
You may use this pen.
💡 Often used as a question to ask for permission.
〜てはいけません
You must not ~
[Verb て-form] はいけません
ここでタバコを吸ってはいけません。
You must not smoke here.
病院で電話をしてはいけません。
You must not talk on the phone in the hospital.
💡 Strong prohibition. Often seen on signs or rules.
Grammar Guide 4

Obligation & State (義務・状態)

〜なければなりません
Must do ~ / Have to ~
[Verb ない-form, drop い] ければなりません
明日、早く起きなければなりません。
I must wake up early tomorrow.
薬を飲まなければなりません。
I must take medicine.
💡 Double negative construction literally meaning 'if you don't do, it's not okay'.
〜なくてもいいです
Don't have to ~
[Verb ない-form, drop い] くてもいいです
明日は学校へ行かなくてもいいです。
I don't have to go to school tomorrow.
💡 The exact opposite of なければなりません.
〜ています
Currently doing ~ / State of being
[Verb て-form] います
今、本を読んでいます。
I am reading a book right now.
私は結婚しています。
I am married. (State)
💡 Can mean an action in progress (I am eating) OR a continuous state (I live in Tokyo).
Grammar Guide 5

Desire & Ability (願望・可能)

〜たいです / 〜ほしいです
Want to do / Want a thing
[Verb stem] たいです
[Noun] がほしいです
日本へ行きたいです。
I want to go to Japan.
新しい車がほしいです。
I want a new car.
💡 たい attaches to verbs. ほしい acts like an adjective taking the object with が.
〜ことができます
Can do ~ / Ability
[Verb Dictionary Form] ことができます
私はピアノを弾くことができます。
I can play the piano.
ここで切符を買うことができます。
You can buy a ticket here.
💡 Formal way to express potential/ability in N5.
Grammar Guide 6

Thoughts & Explanations (思考・説明)

〜から / 〜ので
Because ~
[Reason Phrase] から/ので、[Result]
雨が降っているから、行きません。
Because it's raining, I won't go.
時間がないので、急いでください。
Because there's no time, please hurry.
💡 ので is slightly more formal and objective than から.
〜と思います
I think that ~
[Plain Form Verb/Adj] と思います
明日は晴れると思います。
I think it will be sunny tomorrow.
この映画は面白いと思います。
I think this movie is interesting.
💡 Before と, you must use the plain/casual form (e.g., だ for nouns/na-adjectives).
〜んです
Explanatory tone / Seeking explanation
[Plain Form] んです / のです
どうしたんですか。
What happened? / What's wrong?
頭が痛いんです。
It's that my head hurts.
💡 Used heavily in speech to explain a situation, reason, or ask for a reason.
Grammar Guide 7

Time Relations (時間関係)

〜前に / 〜後で
Before ~ / After ~
[Dict Verb/Nounの] 前に
[た-Form Verb/Nounの] 後で
寝る前に、本を読みます。
Before sleeping, I read a book.
授業の後で、映画を見ます。
After class, I will watch a movie.
💡 前に takes dictionary form. 後で takes た(past) form.
〜てから
After doing ~ (Sequential)
[Verb て-form] から
手を洗ってから、ご飯を食べます。
After washing hands, I eat dinner.
💡 Indicates action B happens immediately or sequentially after action A.
〜ながら
While doing ~
[Verb stem] ながら
音楽を聞きながら、勉強します。
I study while listening to music.
💡 The main action is the second verb.

Warning

Major Exam Traps (試験の罠)

は (wa) vs が (ga)

は

Topic marker. Used for known information.

私が学生です。(As for me, I am a student)

が

Subject marker. Used for new info, or with desires/skills (好き, ほしい).

犬が好きです。(I like dogs)

に (ni) vs で (de)

に

Location of EXISTENCE (います/あります).

部屋に犬がいます。(Dog is in the room)

で

Location of ACTION (食べる/飲む).

部屋で食べます。(Eat in the room)

だけ (dake) vs しか (shika)

だけ

Only. Takes positive verbs.

百円だけあります。

しか

Only. MUST take negative verbs.

百円しかありません。

あります (arimasu) vs います (imasu)

あります

For non-living / immovable things (trees, books).

います

For living / moving things (people, animals).

CRITICAL GRAMMAR

  • 〜てください : Please do
  • 〜てもいいです : May I do?
  • 〜てはいけません : Must not do
  • 〜なければなりません : Must do
  • 〜たことがあります : Have experienced
  • 〜たり〜たりします : Do things like A and B

TRAP PARTICLES

  • [Action Place] で : (公園で走る)
  • [Existence Place] に : (公園にいる)
  • しか ＋ Negative : Only

ADVERBS

  • まだ ＋ Negative : Not yet
  • もう ＋ Past : Already
  • あまり ＋ Negative : Not very
  • ぜんぜん ＋ Negative : Not at all
